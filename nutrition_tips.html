Nutrition Tips!

Looking for more information about how to structure your kitten, adult cat, or senior cat’s diet? Read on for essential nutrition tips to help keep your feline friend healthy.

Nutrients Your Cat Needs

Nutrients are substances obtained from food and used by an animal as a source of energy and as part of the metabolic machinery necessary for maintenance and growth. Here are the six essential classes of nutrients fundamental for healthy living:

  • Water: Ensure your cat always has fresh, clean water available. A deficiency of water can cause serious illness or even death.
  • Proteins: Provide the basic building blocks for cells, tissues, and more. Animal-based proteins have complete amino acid profiles, essential for growth and maintenance.
  • Fats: Essential for energy, hormone production, and more. Omega-6 and omega-3 fatty acids play a vital role in healing inflammation.
  • Carbohydrates: Provide energy for the body’s tissues and are important for intestinal health.
  • Vitamins: Essential for normal metabolic functioning. Most vitamins cannot be synthesized in the body and must be obtained from the diet.
  • Minerals: Essential for bone and teeth structure, fluid balance, and metabolic reactions.

Food to Avoid Feeding Your Cat:

  • Alcohol
  • Chocolate
  • Tea, coffee, and energy drinks
  • Cheese and milk
  • Fat trimmings
  • Raw eggs, raw meat, and raw fish
  • Grapes and their dried forms raisins, sultanas, and currants
  • Onions and garlic
  • Xylitol

Remember to always consult with your veterinarian for specific dietary recommendations tailored to your cat's individual needs.
