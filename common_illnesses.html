Common Cat Illnesses

As a responsible cat owner, it's crucial to be aware of the signs and symptoms of common illnesses in cats so that you can promptly seek veterinary care if needed. Below, we'll explore diseases and medical conditions that frequently affect our feline companions.

Feline Immunodeficiency Virus (FIV)

Feline immunodeficiency virus (FIV) may remain asymptomatic for years after initial infection. However, once the disease progresses, it severely weakens the cat's immune system, making them vulnerable to secondary infections. With proper medical support and a stress-free environment, infected cats can maintain a comfortable quality of life for extended periods.

Feline Leukemia Virus (FelV)

Feline leukemia virus (FeLV) is a transmittable retrovirus that significantly impairs a cat's immune system. It's a leading cause of illness and mortality among domestic cats. Due to its delayed symptom presentation, all new cats entering a household and any sick cats should undergo testing for FeLV.

Heartworm

Heartworm, transmitted by infected mosquitoes, is increasingly recognized as a health concern for domestic cats. Despite its name, heartworm primarily affects the lungs in cats. Prevention is key, especially for cats residing in mosquito-prone areas, and should be discussed with a veterinarian.

High-Rise Syndrome

During warm months, unscreened windows pose a significant risk to cats, often leading to falls known as High-Rise Syndrome. These falls can result in severe injuries such as shattered jaws, punctured lungs, and broken limbs. Pet parents should take precautions to ensure window safety.

Rabies

Rabies is a fatal viral disease affecting the brain and spinal cord of mammals, including cats and humans. It's a preventable disease, and prompt vaccination is essential. Due to its high fatality rate, rabies should be taken seriously, and any potential exposure should be addressed immediately.

Ringworm

Ringworm, caused by a fungus rather than a worm, is highly contagious and affects the skin, hair, and nails. Cats with ringworm may develop circular patches of hair loss with red rings. It's important to treat ringworm promptly to prevent its spread to other pets and humans in the household.

Upper Respiratory Infections

Cats are prone to upper respiratory infections caused by various viruses and bacteria. Symptoms include sneezing, nasal discharge, and coughing. Prompt veterinary care is essential to alleviate symptoms and prevent complications.

Worms

Cats can contract various intestinal parasites, commonly referred to as worms. These parasites can cause a range of symptoms, and some infections may go unnoticed. Regular deworming and veterinary check-ups are essential to maintain your cat's health and prevent potential transmission to humans.

For more information regarding any of these illnesses, please consult a veterinarian or visit the ASPCA's common cat diseases webpage. The link is on the references page.
