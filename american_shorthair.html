History of the American Shorthair Cat

American Shorthairs have a rich history as hardworking rodent catchers. Originally known as Domestic Shorthairs, they earned their new name in 1966 to distinguish them from other shorthair cats and to honor their role in American history. These felines were indispensable on ships like the Mayflower, where they helped control pests during the journey to America. Once ashore, they became valued farm cats, adept at keeping barns and food stores free of rats and mice.

Physical Characteristics

American Shorthairs are renowned for their strength, endurance, and balance, traits honed during their days as colonial mousers. They boast a sturdy and muscular build, coupled with a dense and thick coat that provides warmth, particularly in winter. Despite their cozy fur, the American Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals® (ASPCA®) recommends keeping them indoors for safety reasons, protecting them from contagious illnesses, parasites, and other animals.

These cats typically sport brown or grey tabby coats, although they come in various colors and patterns, including white, black, brown, cream, silver, and blue. Their fur may feature streaks, patches, or solid colors, making each American Shorthair unique.

Common Personality Traits

American Shorthairs are intelligent and observant companions who enjoy watching the world from their vantage points. Affectionate by nature, they relish snuggling and purring in their owners' laps, while also displaying an independent streak. They engage in play on their terms, adding to their charm as loving and playful pets.

5 Fun Facts About the American Shorthair Cat

  • A new name: In 1966, the breed officially became known as the American Shorthair to differentiate it from other shorthaired cats.
  • Mayflower felines: American Shorthairs were present on the historic Mayflower, showcasing their enduring role in American history.
  • Not your typical shorthair: Unlike Domestic Shorthairs, American Shorthairs are purebred, distinguished by their lineage.
  • A cat of many colors: With up to 60 or more coat combinations, American Shorthairs exhibit a wide range of colors and patterns.
  • Look at those peepers! Known for their big, wide eyes, American Shorthairs have distinctive almond-shaped eyes, adding to their charm.
